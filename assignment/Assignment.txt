EMBEDDED INTELLIGENCE
PARTIAL EXAM ASSIGNMENT: INDOOR NAVIGATION WITH SMARTPHONE, BLE BEACONS, AND IMU

Final project assignment for the Master's program in AI. The task combines data collection, sensor fusion, indoor positioning, structured experimentation, and continuous presentation of progress.
Keywords: Indoor Navigation, BLE, IMU, Kalman Filter, Particle Filter, Jupyter Notebook

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. CONTEXT AND GENERAL CONDITIONS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In the third and final project of the module, you will develop and evaluate a method for indoor navigation inside a building based on smartphone sensor data and Bluetooth beacons. This assignment is intended for students in the Master's program in AI and requires a methodologically sound, technically justified, and experimentally validated solution.

In the test area, eight Bluetooth beacons are installed across two floors of the EMI building. The positions of the beacons are known, and received beacon messages can be uniquely assigned to individual beacons.

For data acquisition, you are provided with a smartphone app that records the following data at regular intervals:
- Bluetooth data from received beacons
- Raw IMU data
- Filtered IMU data

The recording app is intended for Android devices. An APK file will be provided. To install it, please enable Developer Mode on the device. The source code of the app will also be provided.

During data recording, the smartphone should be kept in the user's trouser pocket.
The assignment must be completed in teams of three.
The expected workload is approximately 40 hours per person. Every team member must be familiar with the complete project.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. TASK DESCRIPTION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Develop, implement, and evaluate a method for estimating the position and/or the traversed path of a person inside the building. The basis for this task is the smartphone sensor and beacon data, the known beacon infrastructure, and structural knowledge about the building.

Conduct four structured measurement runs in the test area. The selected paths should cover different movement patterns, direction changes, signal conditions, and at least one floor transition. In addition, make sure that the four test runs start from different starting positions.

As position references, you may use the doors of the laboratories in the test area. Stop briefly at selected doors and record the time at which you were at that location. These events serve as reference points for the later interpretation and evaluation of your position estimates. Analyze the first run before conducting additional runs and make sure that the resulting datasets are usable.

4. METHODOLOGICAL REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In your work, you must explicitly use exactly one method from the lecture. You may choose between the following approaches:
- Kalman filter or an appropriate Kalman filter variant
- Particle filter

Your choice of method must be explicitly justified. Discuss the selected method with regard to its assumptions, strengths, weaknesses, and suitability for the given indoor navigation problem.

The use of sensor fusion is mandatory. In particular, the following information sources should be combined:
- Step detection from raw acceleration data to determine individual steps
- Movement direction to derive an error-prone motion sector for the likely new area of presence
- BLE beacon information, where signals with high signal strength should be weighted more strongly than distant or weaker signals
- Knowledge about the building structure, which can be extracted from the attached map and incorporated into the position estimation

Pay particular attention to the fact that the building structure is a central modeling challenge. Walls, corridors, doors, stairs, and transitions between floors strongly constrain plausible positions and movements. These constraints must be explicitly considered in both your modeling and your evaluation.

Also reflect on the fact that such spatial restrictions can often be modeled more directly and flexibly in a particle filter than in a classical Kalman filter, because invalid states can be discarded or down-weighted more easily.
